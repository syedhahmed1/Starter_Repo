## Case 1 — Content review ranking (FlyRank ML, Lane 2)

### The problem

Content teams have more pages than review time. An editor or SEO reviewer can’t open every page. They need a short “look at these first” list — pages that look stale, declining, or worth a refresh — not another pile of charts.

### What I did (and decided)

I chose Lane 2: refresh / content opportunity scoring. I framed the work around a real decision — which pages to review first — not “train a model for its own sake.” I checked the starter search data to confirm the problem shows up in the numbers (lots of pages; some look like strong review candidates). I decided early that this is decision-support: help a human prioritize. It is not proof that editing a page will bring traffic back.

### What came of it

I have a clear problem, lane, and research question a hiring manager can follow. I also have measured signals that the backlog problem is real. I do not have a “traffic went up X%” claim yet — that would be dishonest at this stage. Next, I keep the same limit: claim only that the work helps prioritize review, not that a refresh fixes traffic.
